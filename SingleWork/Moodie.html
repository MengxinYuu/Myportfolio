Background

Most international students have been through a tough time for adaptation, when emotional disorders including depression and anxiety occurs easily. However, some of them don't realize the emotional disorders lying behind their daily emotional changes. Though aware of the problems, part of them hesitate to talk to psychologist, their family and friends, which increases pressure in turns.

Mood tracking proves to be a good way for self-regulation. We propose Moodie as a powerful tool designed for international students to track their mood in an easy way. With Moodieâ€™s supports, international students will fell comfortable and convenient to collect, share and reflect on their daily moods.

Competitive Analysis

To Study current mood tracking apps in the US industry and learn from their advantages and disadvantages, we did a competitive analysis on 8 most famous mood tracking apps in the US industry. Three major dimensions were employed in evaluation: usage (basic features), desired features (important features), and motivation(valuable features matching with user goals).

Collecting

Mood sometimes is hard to tell and specify in words. Although some apps utilize non-verbal cues such as emojis and selfies, the method has the potential to be improved.

Sharing

The feedback users got from their sharing process is just simple response such as heart/hug icons or short sentences, which is non-empathetic.

Reflecting

Although most app help users to record their mood, it mostly for venting but not reflection. The current basic visualization of mood records can not well-support users to generate insights from their mood data, find emotion patterns and improve emotion management.
