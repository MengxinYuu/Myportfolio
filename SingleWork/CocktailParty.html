Cocktail Party

Cocktail introduction, classification and recommendation for novices. Easy to look at and fun to explore :)

Domain

Motivation

The idea to make a website for cocktail introduction, classification and recommendation sparks in a school orientation. Most students including me had no experiences with cocktail and had no idea about what to order. When being asked what I wanted, after a long, awkward silence I just said “the same one with my friend’s”.

For all the uninitiated like me, staring down a menu of cocktails can be an intimidating prospect. What exactly is a Screwdriver? What is inside a Manhattan? Does Long Island Iced Tea really taste like tea? I want something sweet and juicy, is Caipiriha a smart choice?

We propose Cocktail Party to the people coming to their first social event as a gateway into the world of cocktail. Cocktail Party provides the uninitiated with cocktail introduction, classification and recommendation based on their preference, taste and mood.

Design Process

Data processing

Data Collection: We selected 20 most popular cocktails and collected data about ingredient, portion, type, flavor and strength from videos, existing websites and bartenders' blogs.

Data Storage: After cleaning up all the data we had, we storaged our data in Firebase through Jason file.

Key screens mock up




illustration design

We found 25 common ingredients and coded each ingredient with a gradient color.

We divided cocktails we had into three types: Short drink, Long drink and shots and we identified three types of cocktail glassware.

All the cocktails were finished through the combination of color and type of glass.
